1. It is the dog most similar to the wolf

The husky is probably one of the dogs that most reminds us of the wolf, probably because of its pointy ears, penetrating gaze, and pronounced muzzle. Of course, remember that recent studies indicate that the dog does not descend from the wolf, but they are close relatives.

However, the Siberian husky is smaller than these large predators, since that the husky reaches around 56 and 60 centimeters in height at the withers, while wild wolves can measure between 80 or 85 centimeters tall at the withers.

3. Adapts wonderfully to different environments

The husky is a dog that adapts without problems to cold and icy climates: its coat is testimony to its origins in Siberia. However, surprisingly, the husky is also capable of adapting to temperate climates, unlike other Nordic dogs, such as the Alaskan malamute, which usually suffers intense heat.

The husky sheds its coat twice a year, once between spring and summer and once between fall and winter. However, between the two moults, hair loss can also occur, always in small amounts. In the case of observing more loss than normal, it is advisable to go to the vet to rule out allergies and other health problems.

4. His vocalization ability is unique

The husky is a particularly talkative dog, capable of emitting a variety of sounds. It also stands out for its howl, which can be heard up to 15 kilometers away. Some huskies appear to sing, talk, and even whine, however, they do not usually bark.

6. The snow dog

It's no secret that huskies love snow. Virtually all individuals show some interest in it, probably because of the profound impact this element has had on their history. Perhaps for this reason they are also attracted to water and leaf litter in autumn.

7. They are born to run

Along with the Chukchi tribe, huskies worked as sled dogs, carrying food and supplies from one place to another, and contrary to popular belief, huskies did not usually transport people. They were chosen to carry out this task for various reasons, such as resistance to cold, but mainly their ability to make long journeys stood out. The sled was towed by about twenty dogs and each of them performed a specific function.

8. They fit with different types of families

The network is full of cute and funny videos of husky dogs, why is that? It is undoubtedly an excellent playmate for children, a member of the team when going on excursions or a sensitive and affectionate dog on a daily basis. Its character is unique and variable, so much so that you must strive to reinvent yourself and offer different forms of entertainment.

9. Was the husky a war dog?

Probably if we think of war dogs, the story of the German shepherd comes to mind, used as a messenger, rescue dog and even as a "mine" dog. However, the husky also stands out in the Second World War, carrying out transport and communication tasks.

10. Balto, an unprecedented hero

By far, the story of Balto, a mixed-breed husky, is one of the most impressive surrounding the breed, in fact, his popularity was such that Disney released two films explaining his story.

It all starts in 1925, when a large number of children in a town called Nome, Alaska, contracted diphtheria. Faced with the impossibility of receiving the necessary medicines, a group of men together with their dogs decided to undertake a dangerous journey to save the lives of the town's children.

Some men and dogs died, including the guide dogs, however, Balto was the one who took command of the race, despite having no previous experience as a leader. Fortunately, after five and a half days, they reached their destination. The dogs were hailed as heroes and were featured in newspapers across the country.
